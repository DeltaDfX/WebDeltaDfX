What ‘INNOVATIVENESS’ really means??

Innovativeness forms a critical element of innovation and entrepreneurship and relates to the search and development of “creative, unusual or novel solutions to problems and needs”i. This can relate to the introduction of new processes, technologies, products or services, driven by the business or the individual employeeii.

The level of innovativeness is obviously determined by the extent to which the business emphasizes the search and development of these new solutions. This may be driven from external pressures, such as the introduction of a new disruptive technology (i.e. the shared economy and peer-to-peer sharing for the transportation and hospitality sector), the intensified international competition brought about by globalisation, or even government (de)regulation. Internally, it may also emerge from cost pressures for improved profitability, or the adoption of new strategies emphasizing diversification or growth from existing product lines.

Whatever the origin, embracing innovativeness within the business and at individual employee level is critical for driving change and competitiveness. The possibilities to emerge from higher levels of innovativeness range from the introduction of new products or services to existing customers; new processes to achieve existing tasks or business functions more efficiently and effectively; the expansion into new international markets that have not been exploited by your business; or, even through to changing the fundamental business model of the business redefining the entire market offeringiii.

A range of important business characteristics play a role in increasing innovativeness, includingiv:

  • Having CEOs who were heavily involved in fostering innovation
  • Defining innovation as critical to long-term company success
  • Attaching great importance to the concept of managing change
  • Having the words innovation and creativity in the mission statement
  • Demonstrating an openness to outside ideas
  • Having formal programs for idea generation and problem solving
  • Placing strong emphasis on cross-functional communications

When considering developing the innovativeness at the employee level, a range of characteristics should be encouragedv, including:

  • Work behaviours that lead to idea generation, introduction and use
  • Clear rewards for individuals and teams that are innovating within the business
  • Providing resources to employees to dedicate time and money to generating new ideas
  • Individual performance metrices that are linked with the generation and exploitation of new ideas

The most innovative businesses have been shown to be customer- and employee-centered, with focus on new product strategies, and see innovation as an activity based in discipline-specific rules and procedures.
